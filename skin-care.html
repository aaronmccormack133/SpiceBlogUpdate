So how did I get this ugly shit on my face?

Let me introduce you to sebum and his ugly ass. First off all sebum is important for one self as it releases the bad stuff from our body through pores all over us. However these pores may get blocked from all the nasty stuff coming out of it.

This blockage causes spots, smh pores you had one job. The spots that are made are: White heads (under the skin) and blackheads (on the skin). Sebum is commonly built up in hair follicles and from here it releases its stuff at a quick rate, similarly to that of Future dropping mixtapes/albums recently.

Hormones can play apart In this, growing up makes your sebum go into overdrive. Bacteria is also a cause of the development of spots, we can never win.

But what a time to be alive cause I got some tips to save your face.


Mobile Phones


image/pixmania.ie


Yes, our one true friend, our god, the holy grail, may be giving you spots. How could something so innocent do something so bitter you ask?. Well how often do you actually clean your phone thoroughly?.

As we go through our day we are just surrounded by bacteria, and the problem is we’re touching our phones constantly, which means the bacteria on our hands from surfaces such as canteens in work/college, public toilets, you name it get onto the screens without even crossing our minds.

Putting your phone down with the screen side not facing you, this is an example of the bacteria going on to the screen, that bacteria can then spread to your face when you put your phone anywhere near it.

Tips to survive this pandemic: talk hands free as much as possible this will obviously reduce the risk of bacteria spreading.

Clean your phone daily with wipes that you can pick up rather cheaply at a local tech shop or online


Shaving


image/amazon


Your method of shaving may be causing needless spots, or "shaving acne" as its described. These spots may be more itchy and irritating than usual giving off a more swollen type took on your face. Not pleasant.

One of the causes of this is the razor blade that you are using, is it electric or non electric?. Electric razors have less of a chance of giving you some irritated spots on your face as the facial hair isn’t pulled as much as using a regular blade. So an investment for an electric razor may be a wise decision on your behalf.

Tips around this is to have a follow up, like an after shaving procedure using after shave cream that is sensitive and helps soothes the bumps, or what I found helpful was using benzoyl peroxide. Any benzoyl peroxide up to 5% should do the job.


Sleeping


image/ikea


Sleeping may be giving you spots, and no its not how your sleeping, it’s the environment in which you are sleeping in. We wash our clothes quite regularly to make them smell fresh and look elegant, though how many times do you wash your pillowcases?.

Pillowcases like everything can build up dirt and all types of bacteria. Hair products, skin products and just germs have accumulated on the cases, which we may be unaware of. The dirt and germs can spread to our faces as we sleep making us get unnecessary unpropitious spots.

Literally stay woke for the rest of your life fam.

A tip to get over this is to obviously be more aware of the surroundings that your bed resides in, clean away dust of bedside lockers regularly and wash your pillow cases once or twice a week. Easy.


Hair Products


image/Hanz De Fuko


Hair is a key contributor to getting spots. Having your hair down while doing exercise may cause old hair products to drop from your strands of hair onto your face leading to a reaction and outbreak.

Hair products are apart of a man’s everyday essential necessities to help us complete an outfit. However many products that we use are crummy and too cheap for our hair. Not good. Hair products that consist of oil, silicon and whatever horrible based shit is so not good for us.

Using cheap hair products clog up the sweat pores and increases all the spot mutations around our face. So keep in mind what haircut you want next.

Tips around this would be to read descriptions of hair products and only buy products that endorse natural ingredients.

Buying an expensive face cream will not automatically just help your skin, well it might, but chances are slim. Test the market, buy different products and use them for one month each to get a true evaluation of what works best for you. Different creams might react abnormally to your face and could cause even more of an outbreak. R.I.P.
